How do we stop climate change?

  • Illustration by Sue Spevack

The earth is slowing getting hotter because of the way humans live. The warming of the earth leads to extreme weather and rising sea levels, endangering our life on this planet. Some of the things we do, like driving cars and farming cows, release gases like carbon dioxide into the earth's atmosphere which traps the sun's heat, creating a greenhouse effect.

Greenhouses

Greenhouses work by trapping heat in to create warmer conditions for growing plants. The same effect happens when we release carbon dioxide into our atmosphere. In the day, warmth from the sun enters our atmosphere. At night earth cools again as warmth escapes out of the atmosphere. But greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide help to trap the heat in our atmosphere. Over many years, this slowly warms our earth.

What does this mean?

The increase in earths temperature affects people and wildlife.

We more regularly experience extreme weather events like floods and fires which can be very dangerous. Carbon dioxide makes our oceans more acidic which kills sea life and polluted their environment. Gases also pollute the air which we breathe, causing people breathing problems and other health risks.

The ice at both poles is melting, destroying the habitat of animals like polar bears. And sea levels are rising, risking coastal communities and even more flooding.

What are the main causes

The increase in greenhouse gases is mostly caused by:
  • burning fossil fuels for energy
  • agriculture
  • deforestation
  • manufacturing

But we can do something about it. If humans everywhere do the right thing, we can reduce the amount of greenhouse gases we produce. Here are 5 things you can do to help stop climate change. People might tell you one person can’t make a difference because climate change is too big. But if enough people change how they live, we can make a huge difference.

Green travel

Driving everywhere isn’t good for the environment. Cars release carbon into the atmosphere which contributes to global warming. A lot of journey's don’t need a car. Walk, cycle or take public transport instead. It’s better for the environment and better for your health and happiness too.

Eat less meat and diary

Meat production has the single biggest impact on the environment.

Farming for milk, cheese and butter emits considerably more greenhouse gases then producing cereals and vegetables.

Reduce energy use

TVs, computers, lights, phones and heating all need energy to work. The things we buy also need to be manufactured which uses energy. We might consider a pair of jeans to be innocent. But cotton production is incredibly damaging to the environment.
