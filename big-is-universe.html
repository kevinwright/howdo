How big is the Universe?

  • Images courtesy of NASA and unsplash

Measuring in space

Normal measurements like miles don't work in space. Everything is too big. So scientists use the distance light travels in one year to measure distances in space.

Light travels at a speed of 186,282 miles per second so in a year travels 5.8 trillion miles. So a light year measures 5.8 trillion miles.

Earth

To understand the size of the universe and how big a light year is, we need to start with our home. Earth.

Earth is the only planet in the known universe that has life. It is 24,900 miles around at the equator.

To understand how fast light travels, at the speed of light you could travel around the Earths equator 7 times in one second.

Our solar system

Solar System's are planets and stars bound together by gravity. Earth shares a solar system with 7 other planets and a star we call the sun.

Our solar system is big but compared to the universe, it is still tiny.

The size of our Solar System is still too small to use light years to measure distance. But we can use the distance light travels in minutes. It would take light 8 minutes to travel from the sun to earth.

The milky way

A galaxy is a group of solar systems, stars and planets bound together by gravity. Galaxies contain billions of stars.

Our solar system lives in the milky way galaxy. To get a better idea of the size of the milky way, our sun is one of around 400 billion stars that live in the milky way.

The milky way is around 150,000 to 200,000 light years across. So if our solar system was the size of a coin in our hand, the milky way would be the size of the United States.

Local group

The milky way is part of a bigger group of galaxies which scientists have named the local group. This local group is 10 million light years across. Compared with our solar system which we can measure in light minutes, it would take millions of years for light to travel from one side of this group of galaxies to the other. Even at this size, the local group is a part of a bigger cluster of galaxies called a supercluster.

Laniakea Supercluster

The Laniakea Supercluster contains 100,000 galaxies and is 520 million light years across. Laniakea is the supercluster that contains our galaxy the Milky Way.

It is one of 5 superclusters containing millions of galaxies which make up the limit of what we as humans have discovered of the universe we live in. All the planets, stars and galaxies that we know about only make up 4 percent of the universe.
