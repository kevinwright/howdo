Earth

To understand the size of the universe, we need to start with our home. Earth.

Earth is the only habitable planet in the known universe. It is 24,900 miles around at the equator.

Measuring in space

Metres, miles, yards. Those measures won't work in space. Everything is too big, so scientists use something called a light year. From the name, you might think light years measure time but it actually measures distance.

A light year is the distance light travels in one year. Light travels at 186,282 miles per second so a light year measures 5.8 trillion miles.

To put that in perspective, at the speed of light, you could travel around the Earth 7.5 times, in one second.

Our solar system

Solar System's are planets and stars bound together by gravity. Earth shares a solar system with 7 other planets and a star. We call the star in our solar system the sun.

The size of our Solar System means we still can't use light years to measure distances. But we can use light minutes and hours. The distance of the planets from the sun measured by the speed light travels is:

Planet Distance from the sun measured by speed of light
Mercury 3 minutes
Venus 6 minutes
Earth 8 minutes
Mars 13 minutes
Jupiter 44 minutes
Saturn 1.4 hours
Uranus 2.7 hours
Neptune 4.3 hours
