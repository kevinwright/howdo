Measuring in space

Metres, miles, yards. Those measures won't work in space. Everything is too big. So scientists use something called a light year to measure distances in space.

A light year is the distance light travels in one year. Light travels at 186,282 miles per second so a light year measures 5.8 trillion miles.

Earth

To understand the size of the universe and how big a light year is, we need to start with our home. Earth.

Earth is the only habitable planet in the known universe. It is 24,900 miles around at the equator.

To put that in perspective, you could travel around the Earths equator 7 times in one second at the speed of light.

Our solar system

Let's take a look at the size of our solar system.

Solar System's are planets and stars bound together by gravity. Earth shares a solar system with 7 other planets and a star we call the sun. The size of our Solar System is still too small to use light years to measure distance. But we can use light minutes and hours. It would take light 8 minutes to travel from the sun to earth.

The distances from our sun to the planets around it

Planet Distance from the sun measured by speed of light
Mercury 3 minutes
Venus 6 minutes
Earth 8 minutes
Mars 13 minutes
Jupiter 44 minutes
Saturn 1.4 hours
Uranus 2.7 hours
Neptune 4.3 hours

The milky way

A galaxy is a group of solar systems, stars and planets bound together by gravity. Galaxies contain hundreds of millions of stars.

Our solar system lives in a galaxy called the milky way.
